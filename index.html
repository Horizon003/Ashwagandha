02 — Heritage

Historical Background

```
3000+ BCE

Vedic Origins

First recorded use in ancient Ayurvedic medicine. Classified as a prime Rasayana herb for longevity and vitality.

~600 BCE

Charaka Samhita

Extensively documented in Charaka Samhita as a powerful rejuvenating tonic and nervine tonic for stress and weakness.

~600 BCE

Sushruta Samhita

Recommended in Sushruta Samhita for aphrodisiac properties, fertility support, and physical endurance.

Etymology

"Smell of Horse"

"Ashwa" = horse, "Gandha" = smell. The name refers to the root's distinctive odor and the belief it imparts horse-like strength and virility.

Rasayana Classification

Rejuvenative Tonic

Classified in the highest Rasayana category — herbs that promote longevity, enhance memory, and restore youthful energy.

Modern Era

Global Recognition

500+ peer-reviewed clinical studies validate traditional uses. Now a $1B+ global botanical market leader in adaptogens.
```

06 — Science

Pharmacological Actions

```
🧘
Adaptogenic & Anti-Stress
Primary Action
▼

Withanolides modulate the HPA (hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal) axis, reducing serum cortisol levels. Clinical RCTs demonstrate 27.9% reduction in cortisol, 44% reduction in stress scores, and significant improvements in Perceived Stress Scale (PSS) and General Health Questionnaire (GHQ) measures after 60 days of use.

🩸
Anti-Diabetic Potential
Metabolic Action
▼

Withaferin A and Withanolide D stimulate glucose uptake by activating GLUT4 transporters, comparable to metformin in animal models. Reduces fasting blood glucose, HbA1c, and insulin resistance. Anti-oxidative protection of pancreatic β-cells has also been demonstrated.

🧠
Neuroprotective & Anxiolytic
CNS Action
▼

Sitoindosides and Withanolide A promote neurite outgrowth and synaptogenesis in cortical neurons. GABA-mimetic activity explains anxiolytic effects. Reduces amyloid-β plaques in Alzheimer's models. Protects against glutamate-induced neurotoxicity and oxidative stress in brain tissue.

❤️
Cardio-Protective Effects
Cardiovascular Action
▼

β-Sitosterol reduces LDL cholesterol absorption. Withaferin A inhibits platelet aggregation and reduces arterial inflammation. Documented reduction in triglycerides (11%), LDL (up to 17%), and systolic blood pressure in hypertensive patients. Antioxidant protection of cardiomyocytes against ischemia-reperfusion injury.

🛡️
Anti-Inflammatory & Antioxidant
Protective Action
▼

Withaferin A strongly inhibits NF-κB, COX-2, and iNOS pathways. Increases superoxide dismutase (SOD), catalase, and glutathione peroxidase activity. Reduces lipid peroxidation markers. Anti-tumor activity linked to pro-apoptotic and anti-angiogenic mechanisms targeting cancer cell lines in vitro and in vivo.

```
